Key Risk Factors

1. Trading Losses

Trading financial instruments carries substantial risk of loss. Even with proper training and strategies, you may experience significant losses. Factors that can lead to losses include:

  • Market volatility and unexpected price movements
  • Economic events and news releases
  • Geopolitical events
  • Liquidity issues and gaps in pricing
  • Leverage and margin calls
  • Personal emotional decisions and psychological factors

2. Prop Firm Challenge Risk

Participating in proprietary firm challenges has unique risks:

  • Challenge Failure: You may fail to meet challenge objectives despite following training
  • Rule Violations: Accidental violation of prop firm rules can result in disqualification
  • Account Drawdown Limits: Reaching maximum loss limits will result in immediate account termination
  • Profit Target Pressure: Rushing to meet profit targets can lead to poor trading decisions
  • Third-Party Risk: Prop firms may change rules, withdraw offers, or close without notice

3. Past Performance No Guarantee

Past performance does not guarantee future results.

  • Historical trading results shown in examples or testimonials may not be repeated
  • Market conditions change constantly
  • What worked in the past may not work in the future
  • Individual results vary significantly
  • Survivor bias: You may only see success stories, not failures

Results Vary Per Trader

Individual trading results depend on many factors:

  • Experience Level: Beginners face steeper learning curves
  • Discipline: Following rules and risk management is critical
  • Psychological Factors: Emotional control directly impacts results
  • Market Conditions: Some traders excel in trending markets, others in ranging
  • Risk Management: Proper position sizing and stop-loss usage are essential
  • Time & Effort: Active learning and practice significantly affect outcomes

Responsible Trading Guidelines

Before trading with real money, please consider:

  • Start Small: Begin with small position sizes while learning
  • Risk Management: Never risk more than 1-2% of your account per trade
  • Use Stop Losses: Always protect yourself with predetermined exit levels
  • Avoid Leverage: High leverage amplifies both gains and losses
  • Emotional Control: Don't make trading decisions based on emotions
  • Continuous Learning: Markets change; keep upgrading your skills
  • Know Your Limits: Don't trade if you can't afford the risk
  • Have a Plan: Trading without a plan is gambling
